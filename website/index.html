About Graham County DSA

The largest socialist organization in America, organizing for working people in rural Arizona

The Democratic Socialists of America (DSA) is the largest socialist organization in the United States, with over 85,000 members nationwide. We're building a chapter right here in Graham County to bring working people together to fight for economic and social justice in Arizona's Legislative District 19. Source: DSA Official Website, 2025

We believe that working people‚Äîminers, educators, healthcare workers, ranchers, and service workers‚Äîshould run both the economy and society democratically to meet human needs, not to make profits for a few. Why This Matters

Legislative District 19 represents a critical challenge for democratic socialists: organizing in a rural, resource-extraction economy where workers shifted from union solidarity to conservative politics after corporate union-busting. But high poverty rates (17.7%), economic anxiety, and corporate dominance create opportunities for class-based organizing that puts working people first.

The Challenges We Face

Building working-class power in a conservative, resource-dependent region

Organizing in LD19 means confronting the realities of rural, conservative Arizona where economic dependence on mining has created political obstacles. But challenges are opportunities for strategic organizing.

üó≥Ô∏è Republican Dominance

LD19 is R+20-30, with overwhelming GOP voter registration. AIRC classifies district as "outside of competitive range." We must build working-class coalitions that transcend party labels and focus on economic justice.

Source: AIRC District Analysis, 2024

‚öíÔ∏è Corporate Control

Freeport-McMoRan dominates Greenlee County's economy (90% of GDP), owns company towns, and funds anti-union politics. The company paid $6.8M in DOJ environmental fines in 2012. We need strategies to organize in corporate-controlled spaces.

Sources: Mining Data / U.S. DOJ Settlement, 2012

üìâ Declining Union Power

Greenlee went from Arizona's most Democratic county (1912-1996) to strongly Republican as unions declined. Historic 1983-1986 mine strike was crushed‚ÄîNational Guard deployed, workers decertified unions in 1984. Arizona union membership is now 3.7% (2024), down from 8.8% peak in 2007-2008. We must rebuild labor solidarity from the ground up.

Sources: BLS Union Statistics 2024 / Wikipedia - Morenci Strike History

üåç Environment vs. Jobs Framing

Democrats lost mining workers by being seen as anti-mining. We need a just transition message that prioritizes workers while addressing climate crisis. Green New Deal with union jobs for miners.

üí∞ High Poverty Despite Mining Wealth

17.7% poverty rate (1.4x national average) despite massive copper profits shows wealth extraction, not sharing. Median income $67,326 is 10% below Arizona average. This is our organizing opportunity: economic populism that challenges corporate power.

Source: U.S. Census Bureau / Data USA, 2023

üèòÔ∏è Geographic Dispersion

Rural, spread-out population (230,476 across 5 counties, 96 precincts) makes organizing harder but not impossible. Digital tools, strategic hubs, and patient community building are essential.

Source: AIRC District Data, 2024

Our Strategy: Economic Populism + Labor Solidarity

We win by organizing around material conditions: wages, healthcare, housing, education. We support miners, ranchers, and workers while demanding they control the industries they build. We rebuild unions. We challenge corporate power. We put working people first. The data is on our side: high poverty, low union density, corporate extraction. Time to organize.
